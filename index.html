Mission

Build the record so the progress cannot be ignored, erased, or forgotten.

This archive should read as a serious public institution: clear in its purpose, disciplined in its presentation, and durable enough to serve students, educators, departments, journalists, and the wider public.

Representation matters. Documentation matters too.

Black Men in Medicine exists to make Black achievement in medicine legible at scale. Important milestones happen every year, but they only become part of the public record when they are preserved with care.

The archive is built to do two things at once: celebrate the people moving the profession forward and create a visible record of progress that can be cited, studied, and shared.

The long-term goal is straightforward: every major specialty, every year, documented with the consistency and clarity required for real institutional memory.
